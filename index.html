Category Rules / Details
Sequence order A, K, Q, J, 10, 9, 8, 7, 6, 5, 4, 3, 2, A
Players and Cards
  • 2-6 players use 2 decks (106 cards)
  • 7+ players use 3 decks (159 cards)
Objective
  • Form valid sequences (runs) and sets (trios)
Sequence / Run
  • A consecutive set of cards in the same suit (example: 5♥, 6♥, 7♥)
Set / Trio
  • Three cards of the same rank but different suits (example: 5♠, 5♥, 5♦)
  • Valid declaration must include at least 2 runs: 1 pure sequence (no joker) + 1 additional run
Wild Cards
  • Turned-up joker and printed jokers act as wild cards
  • Wild cards can substitute for any card except in a pure sequence
Dealing
  • 13 cards are dealt to each player
  • A card is turned up to determine the wild card
  • Players draw and discard cards to complete their hand
Valid Declaration
  • Must include at least 2 runs, with one being a pure sequence
  • Additional combinations (sets or runs) can be made with or without wild cards
  • Combinations cannot overlap
Packing / Dropping
  • First Drop: pay 10 points if dropping before picking a card
  • Middle Drop: pay 40 points if dropping after picking a card
  • If all players except one drop, the remaining player automatically wins
Scoring
  • Face Cards (A, K, Q, J, 10): 10 points each
  • Number Cards (2-9): face value
  • Jokers (wild cards): 0 points
  • Cards in valid combinations (runs/sets) do not count for points
  • Maximum points for a losing hand: 80 points
Hand Rummy
  • If a player is dealt 13 valid cards, they can declare “Hand Rummy” on their first turn
  • Other players pay double their unmatched cards’ value
Game Void
  • If the stockpile runs out and no one declares, the game is void
Variations
  • Stakes may be doubled if the turned-up card is a printed joker
  • Some rules treat all Aces as jokers when a printed joker is turned up
  • 21-card rummy is a popular variation with additional rules and bonuses
Tactics
  • Use skill to minimize losses and improve winning chances
  • Decide whether to play or pack based on the initial hand
  • Discard strategically to block opponents or trick them into giving you needed cards
note: rules shown here are just reference notes. scoring inputs above are for tracking points only.
